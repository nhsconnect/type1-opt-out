Choose if information about you, which is held by your GP, can be shared for purposes other than your individual care

Choose if your GP patient information can be shared outside your GP practice for purposes other than your individual care.

Contents

  2. How confidential patient information is used
  3. When your choice does not apply
  4. Make your choice

Your health records contain confidential patient information. This is information that both identifies the patient and includes some information about their medical condition or treatment. It is used to support patient's individual care as well as to improve how the NHS plans and delivers services, and for the development of new and improved treatments.

You can choose to stop your confidential patient information being used for any purpose other than your individual care. You can also make a choice for someone else like your children under the age of 13.

Your choice will only apply to GP practices in England. This does not apply to health or care services accessed in Scotland, Wales or Northern Ireland.

This information is also available in other languages and formats.

Your choice

You have choices as to how your confidential patient information is used.

To stop your GP surgery from sharing your data for anything other than your individual care, you can register a Type 1 opt out by using the Type 1 online service or submitting a Type 1 opt out form to your GP Practice.

If you're happy with your confidential patient information being used for planning and research, you do not need to do anything.

You can change how your patient information is used at any time.

Any choice you make will not impact your individual care.
